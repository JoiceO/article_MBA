This project accompanies a Systematic Literature Review (SLR) on public management actions aimed at mitigating greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions. ### Context

Government action through evidence-based public policies (EBPPs) becomes strategic for mitigating the impacts of climate change, particularly in reducing greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions. This analysis explores:

* **Public Managers (Population ‚Äì P):** how different levels of government act
* **Mitigation Policies (Exposure ‚Äì E):** types of policies implemented
* **GHG Reduction or Elimination (Outcome ‚Äì O):** policy impacts and challenges

   * Methodological approaches
   * Geographic map showing distribution of mentions by continent
   * Level of government analyzed
   * Sector of activity (energy, AFOLU, IPPU, waste)

### Technologies Used

### Main Insights from the Study
The results highlight diverse strategies adopted in the post‚ÄìParis Agreement period, showcasing good practices, impacts, and challenges across different institutional and territorial contexts.
